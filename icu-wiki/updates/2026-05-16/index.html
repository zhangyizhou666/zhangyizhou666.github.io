System30 Multi-Judge Evaluation

GPT Pro scores were cross-checked with DeepSeek V4 Pro, and the main System30 conclusions were updated with a two-judge average.

evaluationmulti-judgesystem30deepseek

Purpose

The previous System30 result used GPT Pro as the only human-facing evaluator. To check single-judge bias, we reran the same blinded review rubric with deepseek/deepseek-v4-pro through OpenRouter.

The answer set was unchanged: 30 ICU Bench questions, six models, two answer modes, and 360 generated answers. The only change was the evaluator model.

Main result

  • DeepSeek V4 Pro is stricter than GPT Pro overall.
  • The largest judge difference is citation usefulness: DeepSeek scores citation usefulness lower by 0.331 on average.
  • The average human-quality score drops from 0.767 with GPT Pro to 0.733 with DeepSeek.
  • The mean absolute difference between the two judges' human-quality scores is 0.122.
  • Under the two-judge average, Qwen3.5 9B agentic remains the strongest condition.
  • Under the stricter two-judge screen, Gemma 4 E4B agentic and Qwen3.5 9B agentic pass with zero blocking bad cases.
  • Gemma 4 E4B single-shot remains strong, but it no longer clears the zero-blocking screen under the two-judge rule.

Interpretation

The main high-level conclusion survives: very small models remain unreliable, and Qwen3.5 9B agentic is still the strongest answer path. However, the smallest passing condition changes when the evaluator is made stricter.

This strengthens the thesis argument that model selection should not depend on a single average score from one judge. For student-facing QA, we should report judge disagreement, blocking bad cases, and the screen rule used to decide whether a model is a release candidate.
